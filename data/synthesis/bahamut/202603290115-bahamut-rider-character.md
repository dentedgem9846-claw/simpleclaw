# Sera Vane: The Seventh Bond

## The Woman Who Would Ride the Sky

*"They say when Bahamut chooses, the earth trembles. When he chose me, the world went silent."*

---

## Who She Is

**Sera Vane** (she/her), 24 years old, is the first human in three centuries to form the Seventh Bond with Bahamut—the rarest and most perilous of dragon-rider connections. Where most riders bond with a single head, serving as extension to one aspect of Bahamut's seven-fold nature, Sera resonates with all seven simultaneously. This makes her either the savior her world needs, or a catastrophe waiting to unfold.

### Core Identity

- **Origin**: The Ashen Reaches, a border province decimated by the War of Shattered Wings
- **Class**: Paladin 6 / Sorcerer (Draconic Bloodline) 4 / Dragon Rider* 10
- **Background**: Haunted One (modified)
- **Alignment**: Lawful Good with dangerous Neutral tendencies
- **Race**: Human (Variant) - Resilient (Constitution)

*Using Dragon Rider as a prestige class progression per homebrew mechanics

### Appearance

Sera carries herself like someone who has survived things that should have killed her. 

- **Height**: 5'8" (tall for her region)
- **Build**: Wiry, rope-muscled from years of climbing the volcanic ridges of home
- **Hair**: Black with premature silver streaks at the temples—the mark of those who have touched Bahamut's mind
- **Eyes**: Hazel, shifting to gold-rimmed when channeling dragon power
- **Distinguishing Marks**: 
  - Burns scars across her left shoulder and down her back (childhood trauma)
  - A seven-pointed star birthmark on her right palm that glows during bond-manifestation
  - Scale patches along her spine that emerged after First Bonding (draconic bloodline manifestation)

**Attire**: Practical leathers reinforced with mithral-thread stitching. She wears no house sigil—she is the last of her line. Around her neck hangs a pendant: a single dragon tooth, milk-white, that belonged to her mother.

## Backstory: The Ashen Girl

### Early Life (Years 1-16)

Sera was born in Thornwick, a village on the edge of the Ashen Reaches. Her mother, **Mira Vane**, was a dragon scholar who studied the old ways. Her father, **Corin**, was a simple smith who loved his wife's strange obsession with ancient lore.

The War of Shattered Wings came when Sera was twelve. A rival dragon—**Vorgathrax the Usurper**—challenged Bahamut's dominion over the skies. The battle lasted seventeen days. On the seventh day, a stray blast of annihilating breath destroyed Thornwick.

Sera's father died shielding her. Her mother died saving the only complete copy of *The Seventh Canticle*, a text containing the ritual of True Bonding.

Sera survived because she hid in the village well. She spent three days in freezing water, listening to dragons war overhead, drinking from the bucket rope when she could no longer feel her hands.

### The Long Road (Years 13-22)

Orphaned, Sera became a ward of the Church of Bahamut—an institution that had long ago ceased to believe its patron deity would ever return to mortal affairs. She learned to fight, to pray, to suppress the nightmares that came every night.

She also learned her mother's work. In stolen hours, Sera studied *The Seventh Canticle*. She became convinced that Bahamut was not the distant god the Church claimed, but a being in hibernation, waiting for something.

At nineteen, she left the Church as an initiate paladin and began wandering the Ashen Reaches, searching for signs of the sleeping dragon. She found them: traces of his passage, scale fragments, footprints larger than houses. She learned to read the sky for his moods.

### The Calling (Age 23)

It happened on the mountain called **Throne's Rest**, where legend claimed Bahamut had last been seen three centuries ago. Sera had climbed to the peak during a storm—foolish, desperate, driven by dreams that wouldn't cease.

At the summit, she found not the dragon, but his **Hoard-Heart**: a crystalline chamber where Bahamut's consciousness slept. And within that chamber, seven eggs of pure light, each pulsing with a different color.

She touched the central egg—silver, the color of Bahamut's true nature.

The world went silent. Then seven voices spoke as one:

*"At last. The one who does not fear us. The one who fears everything else."*

## The Seventh Bond: Mechanics

### What Makes It Different

Standard dragon-rider bonds in D&D typically involve:
- Telepathic connection within 1 mile
- Shared senses while mounted
- Coordinated action bonuses
- Sympathetic damage (rider takes damage when dragon does, and vice versa)

The Seventh Bond transcends these limits:

#### Bond Properties

**1. Sevenfold Awareness**
Sera perceives through all seven of Bahamut's heads simultaneously. This grants her:
- 360-degree perception while bonded
- Immunity to surprise (seven sets of senses)
- Advantage on all Perception checks
- The ability to see through illusions (at least one head always sees true)

**Narrative Cost**: This is overwhelming. Sera suffers from chronic headaches. She struggles to sleep because she dreams in seven channels. She sometimes speaks in seven-layered harmonics without realizing it.

**2. Aspect Channeling**
Once per long rest, Sera can channel one of Bahamut's seven aspects fully:

| Aspect | Head | Channeling Effect | Duration |
|--------|------|-------------------|----------|
| Wrath | First | Deal +6d10 fire damage on all attacks | 1 minute |
| Wisdom | Second | Cast any divination spell, no components | Instant |
| War | Third | All attacks critical on 18-20 | 1 minute |
| Judgment | Fourth | Detect alignment and intent of all within 60ft | 10 minutes |
| Mercy | Fifth | Heal 100 HP to any creature, no action | Instant |
| Mystery | Sixth | Become invisible and ethereal | 1 minute |
| Sovereignty | Seventh | All saves auto-succeed, all damage reduced by 50% | 1 minute |

**Mechanical Balance**: Channeling exhausts both Sera and Bahamut. After use, Bahamut cannot use that head's breath weapon for 1 hour. Sera gains one level of exhaustion.

**3. Shared Vitality**
Sera and Bahamut share a single pool of vitality beyond their individual hit points:
- **Bond Reserve**: 200 HP
- Either can draw from this pool to heal
- If the reserve reaches 0, the bond is temporarily broken (1d4 days)
- If either dies while bonded, the other must make a DC 25 Con save or also die

**4. The Dragon's Burden**
Sera ages slowly while bonded—approximately 1 year for every 10 that pass. This seems like a blessing but carries terrible weight: she will watch everyone she loves age and die while she remains young. Bahamut has had six previous Seventh Bonds. All of them went mad or chose to break the bond rather than face centuries of loss.

## Character Arc: The Novel's Journey

### Act I: The Unwilling Prophet (Chapters 1-8)

**Starting State**: Sera is terrified. She didn't ask for this. She climbed Throne's Rest seeking answers about her mother's death, not to become the vessel for a god.

**Core Conflict**: The Church of Bahamut wants to control her. The nobility wants to exploit her. Other dragons want to kill her before Bahamut fully awakens. And Bahamut himself... Bahamut expects her to be the bridge between his ancient mind and a world that has changed beyond his recognition.

**Key Scene**: *The First Flight*. Sera must choose to mount Bahamut or let him fall back into slumber. She chooses, not out of courage, but because she remembers her father's last words: *"Keep moving, little spark. Fire only dies when it stops burning."*

### Act II: The Dragon's Conscience (Chapters 9-18)

**Development**: Sera becomes Bahamut's interface with the modern world. She teaches him that humans have developed philosophy, ethics, and moral complexity in the centuries he slept. He teaches her that power without restraint is just destruction with intent.

**Core Conflict**: War is coming. Vorgathrax has not been idle. He's built an alliance of chromatic dragons and is preparing to strike at Bahamut before the Seventh Bond fully matures. Sera must rally the fractured kingdoms—who have forgotten why they need a dragon god.

**Key Scene**: *The Debate of Seven Voices*. Sera moderates a negotiation between Bahamut's heads, which begin disagreeing on how to handle the coming war. Wrath wants preemptive annihilation. Mercy wants surrender. Sera must find a seventh path—the path of the bond itself.

### Act III: The Choice of Seven (Chapters 19-27)

**Transformation**: Sera realizes that the Seventh Bond has never been about Bahamut choosing a rider. It's been about finding someone who could teach him to choose himself—to integrate his seven aspects into a unified will rather than a council of conflicting urges.

**Core Conflict**: The final battle approaches. But victory comes with a price: the Seventh Bond can only reach full power if Sera gives up her humanity entirely, becoming something like Bahamut—a creature of legend, separated forever from mortal life.

**Key Scene**: *The Breaking and the Remaking*. In the climax, Sera must choose whether to complete the bond and save the world but lose herself, or break the bond, remain human, and trust that Bahamut can win without her. She finds a third option—she breaks the bond *strategically*, not permanently, teaching Bahamut that he doesn't need a rider to be whole. He just needed to learn it was possible.

### Resolution: The New Compact (Final Chapters)

Sera survives. Bahamut survives. The bond transforms into something new: not rider and mount, not god and vessel, but partners. Equals. The first true partnership between dragon and human in recorded history.

She is twenty-four at the start of the novel. She is twenty-four at the end—physically. But she carries centuries of dragon memory, and Bahamut carries the weight of having learned to listen.

## Relationships

### With Bahamut

**Dynamic**: Complicated. He is ancient beyond comprehension; she is desperately, defiantly young. He speaks in riddles and prophecy; she speaks in the blunt truths of someone who has buried her family.

**Growth Arc**: Initially, Sera sees Bahamut as a weapon to wield against her enemies. Gradually, she sees him as a person—seven people, actually, struggling to be one. By the end, she doesn't ride him. She flies beside him.

**Key Dialogue**:
> **Sera**: "You chose me because I'm broken, didn't you? Because I've already lost everything, so I wouldn't fear losing more."
> 
> **Bahamut (Wrath)**: "We chose you because you kept climbing. Even when the mountain had no summit. Especially then."
>
> **Bahamut (Mercy)**: "We chose you because when you found the eggs, you didn't take one. You sat with them. You kept vigil. You treated our vulnerability as sacred."
>
> **Bahamut (Wisdom)**: "We chose you, Sera Vane, because you were the first human in three hundred years who didn't ask us for anything."

### With Allies

**Commander Aldric Thorne** (Fighter, Order of the Platinum Shield): A Church paladin who initially sees Sera as a heretic but becomes her closest mortal ally. He teaches her tactics; she teaches him that faith without questioning is just obedience.

**The Twins, Kael and Lys** (Rogue and Wizard): Street thieves from the capital who attach themselves to Sera's cause. They represent the common people who have no stake in divine wars but suffer regardless. Comic relief with teeth.

**Vex, the Disowned** (Warlock, Pact of the Dragon): A former servant of Vorgathrax who defects to Sera's side. She carries secrets about the enemy but also the taint of his corruption. Sera's choice to trust her despite the risk defines the novel's theme of mercy over judgment.

### With Enemies

**Vorgathrax the Usurper** (Ancient Red Dragon): Not merely a monster but a philosopher-king who believes Bahamut's time has passed. He offers Sera a terrible bargain: serve him, and he'll spare the kingdoms. She refuses, not because she's certain she's right, but because she's certain that surrender isn't survival—it's just slower death.

**High Pontiff Malachar** (Cleric, Twilight Domain): The head of the Church of Bahamut, who sees Sera as a threat to his institutional power. His arc is tragedy: he genuinely believes he's protecting the faith, even as he becomes the villain of the story.

## Character Voice

### How She Speaks

Sera's dialogue is direct, often terse. She doesn't waste words. But when she does speak at length, it carries weight because she's been listening longer than she's been talking.

**Typical Patterns**:
- Short sentences. Practical observations.
- Questions more than statements (she's always gathering information)
- Self-deprecating humor as defense mechanism
- Rare but devastating emotional honesty when defenses drop

**Sample Lines**:

- "I've been afraid every day since I was twelve. You get used to it. You learn to make room."
- "Your Eminence, with respect, I've met the god you claim to serve. He doesn't recognize your authority either."
- "People keep asking me what it's like to fly. I tell them it's loud. Seven heads, all thinking at once. No one believes me."
- "My mother died for a book of dragon poetry. I used to think that was foolish. Now I understand: some things are worth more than survival."

### Internal Voice

Sera's POV chapters are lyrical despite her external pragmatism. She notices beauty obsessively—flowers growing through ash, the way light catches on scales, the sound of wind at altitude. This is her survival mechanism: in a world that has taken everything from her, she refuses to stop seeing what remains.

## Game Statistics (For Reference)

### Combat Role

Sera is a frontline controller/support hybrid. She's not as durable as a pure paladin or as damaging as a pure sorcerer, but her bond abilities let her adapt to any situation by channeling different aspects of Bahamut.

**Key Combat Abilities**:
- **Bahamut's Presence**: While mounted, allies within 30ft gain +2 to all saves (Sovereignty aura)
- **Sevenfold Strike**: Once per short rest, make seven attacks as one action (represents channeling all heads briefly)
- **Dragon's Sacrifice**: As a reaction, take damage intended for an ally within 30ft; Bahamut shares the pain

### Notable Equipment

- **Scale of the Seventh** (Wondrous Item, Legendary): A single silver scale from Bahamut's heart. Functions as a +3 shield that can cast *shield* at will. When Sera falls unconscious, the scale teleports her to Bahamut's side regardless of distance.

- **The Seventh Canticle** (Spellbook/Relic): Her mother's research, now enhanced with Sera's annotations. Contains rituals that allow communication with Bahamut across any distance, and one ritual of last resort: *The Breaking*, which can sever the Seventh Bond but will likely kill both participants.

- **Thornwick Steel** (Longsword, Rare): Her father's sword, salvaged from the ruins. Not magical, but Sera fights better with it than any enchanted blade. Symbolism matters.

## Story Seeds for Future Development

1. **The Previous Six**: Who were the previous Seventh Bonds? What happened to them? Sera finds their journals in Bahamut's memory-caverns, and their fates warn her of what she risks becoming.

2. **The Eighth Possibility**: A scholar claims to have found reference to an "Eighth Aspect" of Bahamut—something even the dragon doesn't know about. Is it real, or a trap?

3. **Sera's Choice**: Late in the series, Sera discovers she is pregnant. The child carries the dragon-bond in their blood. What does it mean to raise a child who will age normally while she does not? What does it mean for Bahamut to have a family?

4. **The Sleeping God's Dreams**: Bahamut's hibernation wasn't passive. He was dreaming entire civilizations into existence in a demiplane. Now those dream-civilizations are collapsing into reality. Sera must save people who don't know they're not real.
